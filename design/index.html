One of my passions is graphical design. I get to practice this in a professional capacity as, in addition to being a Software Engineer, I also design the icons we use in our tools. This is a job I greatly enjoy as both a break from coding and a creative outlet.

Outside of work I enjoy 3D modeling and rendering. I am for the most part self-taught, although I also took relevant modules in my degree where possible.

For image creation and editing I mostly use Photoshop, although I am currently learning GIMP as an alternative.

For 3D modelling, my older creations used Wings3D for both modeling and texturing, a package I strongly recommend for anyone starting out. I have since moved on to using Maya for both purposes. I have used a variety of rendering packages: the majority of my recent models have been rendered with metal ray, while my older creations were rendered with Kerkythea.

Finished: Ongoing
Description: This is a project I started in August 2013 and have been working on intermittently ever since. The project's aim is to model a 1934 Riley Ulster Imp, a rare sports car typical of the prewar era: a historical period I find fascinating (as further evidenced by the 1934 motorcycle I have also created). I have mostly finished the modelling, although some of the bodywork needs cleaning up and rivets/screw heads need adding. Once this is complete the model will need texturing. My ultimate aim is to texture both this and the motorcycle so I may create a scene containing the pair of them.

1934 600cc Douglas OW1 Motorcycle

  • gallery/motorbike_1.jpg
  • gallery/motorbike_2.jpg
  • gallery/motorbike_3.jpg
Image 0 of 0 ← →
Finished: 18-Nov-2011
Description: This was created in the 3rd year of my degree as part of a Character and Set Design unit and I achieved the highest mark in the year for the model. The assignment was to visit the M Shed, a local modern-history museum, and choose an exhibit to reproduce. I chose a motorcycle which I later found out to be a 1934 600cc Douglas OW1, 1 of 8 remaining in the world. Due to its rarity no blueprints or other reference images were available, so I took plenty of photographs and sketched an orthographic, side-on view from these to use as reference. The assignment was to create a raw model, as we hadn't been taught how to apply textures/materials, hence the lack of colour. This is one of my favourite projects and I'm exceptionally pleased with how it turned out. I intend one day to finish it off by texturing and properly rendering it.

'Olympoid' Character

  • gallery/olympoid_1.jpg
  • gallery/olympoid_2.jpg
  • gallery/olympoid_3.jpg
Image 0 of 0 ← →
Finished: 20-Jan-2012
Description: This character was created in the 3rd year of my degree as part of a Character and Set Design course. I received the third highest mark in the year for the character. The specification was to create a robotic character (or Olympoid) for the 2040 Olympics, when it was posited that robots would be taking part in place of humans and a single robot would be required to compete in all the games. My robot was designed to represent Greece and took influence from the Greek gladiators that would have taken part in the ancient Olympic Games. I'm not 100% happy with the result and believe I could have done better, however I had to rush the final stages, hence the compromises. I may go back to this model at a later date and finish it properly. The texturing in particular could be improved by adding some appropriate damage/wear and fixing the tearing issues visible in places.

'Primitives' Short

Finished: 30-Nov-2012
Description: This short was put together for a project I did in the 4th year of my degree as part of an Animation Production unit. I obtained the second highest mark in the year for the result. The specification was to have many smaller objects come together to form a larger object or character. Created in Maya (with a small amount of post production in After Effects) my short took a somewhat unique twist on the assignment and, rather than bringing many identical or almost identical objects together into a character or creating a character and breaking it apart, I bring together many objects from a more natural environment. My intention with this was to keep a much greater sense of suspense running throughout the short, as well as giving the final character some personality. This is probably one of the largest and most ambitious projects I have undertaken with the entire short being single handedly created from scratch in three weeks while also managing other deadlines.

'Dance' Short

Finished: 24-Jan-2013
Description: This short was put together for a project I did in the 4th year of my degree as part of the Animation Production unit. The specification was to have a character which you downloaded from the internet perform a dance routine to a song of your choice. Created in Maya, rendered in mentalray and composited in After Effects, my project brought a statue to life rather than a more conventional human character, dancing to Michael Jackson's Smooth Criminal. The statue model was downloaded from here, and the island model (a small portion of which is used as a backdrop) was downloaded from here. I achieved the highest mark in the year for the short.
